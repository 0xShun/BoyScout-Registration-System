Boy Scouts of the Philippines

A Legacy of Leadership, Service, and Character Development Since 1923

The Boy Scouts of the Philippines (BSP) has been instrumental in shaping the character and values of millions of Filipino youth since 1923. Through comprehensive programs focused on leadership, patriotism, and community service, BSP continues to empower young Filipinos to become responsible citizens and future leaders of our nation.

October 5, 1923
Founding of BSP

The Boy Scouts of the Philippines was formally established as a branch of the Boy Scouts of America. Founded in Manila by the Rotary Club of Manila, this marked the beginning of organized scouting in the Philippines.

1936
Legal Establishment

The Boy Scouts of the Philippines was legally established through Commonwealth Act No. 111, giving the organization official recognition and support from the Philippine government.

1974
First Major Amendment

Commonwealth Act 111 was amended through Presidential Decree No. 460, modernizing the organization's structure and expanding its mandate.

1992
Second Major Amendment

The BSP charter was further amended through Republic Act No. 7278, strengthening the organization's capacity for youth development.

2006
Inclusion of Girls

BSP issued National Office Memorandum No. 47, Series of 2006, which formally allowed girls aged 13 to 17 years old to join, marking a significant step toward gender inclusivity.

Present Day
Continuing Excellence

Today, BSP continues to thrive as one of the largest youth organizations in the Philippines, actively contributing to nation-building through character development, leadership training, and community service.

"To be the premier voluntary organization in youth development and nation building."

What This Means:
  • Cultivate excellence in youth development
  • Lead by example in character formation
  • Contribute significantly to nation-building efforts
  • Maintain international standards of scouting excellence

"To imbue in the youth and adults the love of God, country, and fellowmen; to train them in character building, citizenship training, and physical, mental, social, moral, and spiritual development."

Core Components:
  • Spiritual Development: Instilling love of God and fostering spiritual growth
  • Patriotism: Cultivating love of country and commitment to national development
  • Fellowship: Promoting brotherhood, compassion, and service to others
  • Holistic Development: Ensuring physical, mental, social, moral, and spiritual growth

Strategic Goals:
  1. Character Development: Develop young people with strong moral character guided by the Scout Oath and Law
  2. Leadership Excellence: Cultivate leadership skills through practical experiences
  3. Citizenship Training: Produce responsible and active citizens committed to democratic values
  4. Life Skills Acquisition: Equip youth with practical skills including outdoor survival, first aid, and problem-solving
  5. Community Service: Foster a spirit of service through community development projects
  6. Global Citizenship: Promote international understanding through world scouting events

  • Service: Dedication to helping others without expecting reward
  • Integrity: Commitment to honesty and strong moral principles
  • Respect: Showing consideration for oneself, others, and the environment
  • Excellence: Striving for the highest standards in all endeavors
  • Unity: Fostering brotherhood and cooperation
  • Stewardship: Responsible care of our natural environment

Key Legal Documents:
Commonwealth Act No. 111 (1936)

Purpose: Foundational charter that legally established the BSP

  • Official recognition as national scouting organization
  • Definition of organizational structure
  • Mandate for character development and citizenship training
Presidential Decree No. 460 (1974)

Purpose: First amendment to modernize BSP's charter

  • Updated organizational structure
  • Expanded mandate for youth development
  • Strengthened government agency partnerships
Republic Act No. 7278 (1992)

Purpose: Second major amendment to strengthen BSP's capacity

  • Modernization of governance structures
  • Enhanced accountability requirements
  • Alignment with international scouting standards
National Office Memorandum No. 47, S. 2006

Purpose: Landmark memorandum allowing female participation

  • Formal permission for girls aged 13-17 to join BSP
  • Guidelines for co-educational scouting activities
  • Equal opportunities for advancement and recognition

BSP operates under a clear mandate to serve the nation through youth development:

  • Educational Partnership: Work with DepEd to complement formal education
  • Nation Building: Produce responsible citizens committed to service
  • International Cooperation: Represent Philippines in World Organization of Scout Movement (WOSM)
  • Environmental Advocacy: Promote environmental awareness and conservation
  • Community Service: Mobilize scouts for disaster response and humanitarian aid
  • Volunteer Development: Train adult volunteers to mentor young scouts
