Boy Scouts of the Philippines

A Legacy of Leadership, Service, and Character Development Since 1923

Empowering Filipino Youth for Over a Century

The Boy Scouts of the Philippines (BSP) has been instrumental in shaping the character and values of millions of Filipino youth, fostering leadership, patriotism, and service to others.

October 5, 1923
Founding of BSP

The Boy Scouts of the Philippines was formally established as a branch of the Boy Scouts of America. Founded in Manila by the Rotary Club of Manila, this marked the beginning of organized scouting in the Philippines.

1936
Legal Establishment

The Boy Scouts of the Philippines was legally established through Commonwealth Act No. 111, giving the organization official recognition and support from the Philippine government. This act provided the legal framework for BSP operations and its role in youth development.

1974
First Major Amendment

Commonwealth Act 111 was amended through Presidential Decree No. 460, modernizing the organization's structure and expanding its mandate to better serve Filipino youth in the changing times.

1992
Second Major Amendment

The BSP charter was further amended through Republic Act No. 7278, strengthening the organization's capacity to address contemporary challenges and opportunities in youth development.

2006
Inclusion of Girls

BSP issued National Office Memorandum No. 47, Series of 2006, which formally allowed girls aged 13 to 17 years old to join the Boy Scouts of the Philippines, marking a significant step toward gender inclusivity in the organization.

Present Day
Continuing Excellence

Today, BSP continues to thrive as one of the largest youth organizations in the Philippines, actively contributing to nation-building through character development, leadership training, and community service.

"To be the premier voluntary organization in youth development and nation building."

What This Means:

The Boy Scouts of the Philippines envisions itself as the leading organization dedicated to developing young Filipinos into responsible citizens and leaders. Through its comprehensive programs, BSP aims to:

  • Cultivate excellence in youth development
  • Lead by example in character formation
  • Contribute significantly to nation-building efforts
  • Maintain international standards of scouting excellence

"To imbue in the youth and adults the love of God, country, and fellowmen; to train them in character building, citizenship training, and physical, mental, social, moral, and spiritual development."

Core Components of Our Mission:
Spiritual Development

Instilling love of God and fostering spiritual growth through faith-based activities and moral guidance.

Patriotism

Cultivating love of country and commitment to national development and service.

Fellowship

Promoting brotherhood, compassion, and service to others in the community.

Holistic Development

Ensuring physical, mental, social, moral, and spiritual growth of every scout.

Strategic Goals:
1. Character Development

To develop young people with strong moral character, guided by the Scout Oath and Law, capable of making ethical decisions and contributing positively to society.

2. Leadership Excellence

To cultivate leadership skills through practical experiences, empowering scouts to lead in their communities, schools, and future careers.

3. Citizenship Training

To produce responsible and active citizens who understand their civic duties and are committed to democratic values and nation-building.

4. Life Skills Acquisition

To equip youth with practical life skills including outdoor survival, first aid, environmental stewardship, and problem-solving abilities.

5. Community Service

To foster a spirit of service through active participation in community development projects and humanitarian activities.

6. Global Citizenship

To promote international understanding and cooperation through participation in world scouting events and cross-cultural exchanges.

Key Legal Documents:
Commonwealth Act No. 111 (1936)

Purpose: The foundational charter that legally established the Boy Scouts of the Philippines as a corporate entity.

Key Provisions:

  • Official recognition of BSP as the national scouting organization
  • Definition of organizational structure and governance
  • Mandate to promote character development and citizenship training
  • Authority to award badges, ranks, and recognitions
Presidential Decree No. 460 (1974)

Purpose: First amendment to modernize BSP's charter during the Marcos administration.

Key Changes:

  • Updated organizational structure to reflect contemporary needs
  • Expanded mandate to address evolving youth development challenges
  • Strengthened ties with government agencies for youth programs
  • Enhanced BSP's role in national development initiatives
Republic Act No. 7278 (1992)

Purpose: Second major amendment to further strengthen BSP's capacity and relevance.

Key Provisions:

  • Modernization of governance structures
  • Enhanced accountability and transparency requirements
  • Broader authority for program development and implementation
  • Alignment with international scouting standards
  • Provisions for sustainable funding and resource management
National Office Memorandum No. 47, S. 2006

Purpose: Landmark memorandum allowing female participation in BSP.

Key Provisions:

  • Formal permission for girls aged 13 to 17 to join BSP programs
  • Guidelines for co-educational scouting activities
  • Safety and supervision protocols for mixed-gender units
  • Equal opportunities for advancement and recognition
  • Commitment to gender equality in youth development
Historical Significance: This memorandum marked a transformative moment in BSP history, demonstrating the organization's commitment to inclusivity and equal opportunity while maintaining its core values and traditions.
Ongoing Development

BSP continues to work with government agencies, including DepEd, to ensure compliance with current regulations and to develop new policies that address contemporary challenges in youth development and education.

The Boy Scouts of the Philippines operates under a clear mandate to serve the nation through youth development:

Educational Partnership

Work closely with the Department of Education (DepEd) to complement formal education with character development and life skills training.

Nation Building

Contribute to national development by producing responsible citizens committed to service and excellence.

International Cooperation

Represent the Philippines in the World Organization of the Scout Movement (WOSM) and promote international understanding.

Environmental Advocacy

Promote environmental awareness and conservation through practical action and education.

Community Service

Mobilize scouts for disaster response, community development, and humanitarian assistance.

Volunteer Development

Train and support adult volunteers to effectively mentor and guide young scouts.

The Scout Oath and Law

Every scout in the Philippines pledges to uphold the Scout Oath and Law, which embody the organization's fundamental principles:

  • Duty to God and country
  • Service to others
  • Personal development and self-discipline
  • Respect for nature and the environment
  • Brotherhood and international friendship
