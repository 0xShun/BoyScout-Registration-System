Our History

1910

The Beginning

The Boy Scouts of the Philippines was founded in 1910, making it one of the oldest youth organizations in the country. It was established to develop the youth's character, citizenship, and personal fitness through the Scout Oath and Law.

1937

Official Recognition

In 1937, the Boy Scouts of the Philippines was officially recognized by Commonwealth Act No. 111, solidifying its role in nation-building and youth development.

Present

Tacloban City Council

The Tacloban City Council continues the proud tradition of scouting, serving the youth of our community with dedication and honor. We focus on building character, developing leadership skills, and fostering a spirit of service among our scouts.

Vision

To be the premier youth organization that develops responsible citizens and leaders committed to the service of God, country, and fellowmen.

Mission

To contribute to the education of young people through a value system based on the Scout Promise and Law, helping build a better world where people are self-fulfilled and play a constructive role in society.

Scout Oath

"On my honor, I promise to do my best:
To do my duty to God and country,
To help other people at all times,
And to obey the Scout Law."
