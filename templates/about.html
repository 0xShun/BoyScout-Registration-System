About Boy Scouts of the Philippines

Tacloban City Council - A Legacy of Service, Honor, and Community

Official Memorandum

The Boy Scouts of the Philippines (BSP) was officially established as a public corporation by Commonwealth Act No. 111 in 1936. This act legally recognized and institutionalized the scouting movement in the Philippines, providing it with a mandate to develop the character and citizenship of Filipino youth through outdoor education, values formation, and community service.

Core Mission

The BSP operates under the principles of the Scout Oath and Law, promoting patriotism, self-reliance, and social responsibility among Filipino youth. Through its structured programs for Cub Scouts, Boy Scouts, Senior Scouts, and Rover Scouts, the organization continues to shape future leaders and responsible citizens.

Our History

Pre-World War II Era

Early Presence in Leyte (Pre-WWII)

While the BSP's roots in the Philippines date back to 1910, the local history of Scouting in the Tacloban region is marked by significant milestones. An early marker of the movement's presence in the region is the Boyscout Monument in Palo, Leyte (a neighboring town), which was erected in 1941.

Historic Landmark

This monument is noted as the first-ever Boyscout monument in human form in the country, demonstrating the strong foundation of scouting in the area before the war.

October 1944

World War II Heroism - Battle of Leyte

One of the most significant and heroic chapters in Leyte's BSP history occurred during the Battle of Leyte in October 1944. This event stands as a testament to the courage, quick thinking, and selflessness of Filipino Boy Scouts during one of the most critical moments in Philippine history.

The Heroes of Tolosa

Three Boy Scouts from Tolosa, Leyte (near Tacloban) used semaphore flag signaling to warn incoming Allied forces and divert shelling away from populated areas of Tolosa. Their heroic action is credited with saving thousands of civilian lives.

This act of valor is now commemorated annually in the province on "Signal Day", ensuring that the bravery of these young scouts is never forgotten.

Modern Era

The Tacloban City Council

The Tacloban City Council (TCC) of the BSP operates as a distinct unit under the Eastern Visayas Region, actively implementing the scouting programs for:

  • Cub Scouts - Developing young minds through fun and adventure
  • Boy Scouts - Building character and leadership skills
  • Senior Scouts - Advancing outdoor skills and community service
  • Rover Scouts - Mentoring and leading the next generation

The council continues to uphold the values of the Scout Oath and Law while serving the Tacloban City community through various programs and activities.

November 2013 - Present

Resilience After Typhoon Yolanda (Haiyan)

The organization demonstrated its resilience following the devastation of Super Typhoon Yolanda (Haiyan) in November 2013. The typhoon destroyed much of Tacloban City, including the building of the BSP Leyte Council.

Rebuilding Together

With support from international partners like the World Scout Foundation, the organization successfully oversaw the reconstruction of a new office building. This achievement highlighted their commitment to rebuilding and continuing service in the community, even in the face of unprecedented challenges.

Today

Current Activities & Community Engagement

The Tacloban City Council remains highly active in the community, demonstrating the enduring spirit of scouting through various initiatives:

  • Participating in major civic events such as Independence Day celebrations
  • Conducting core scouting programs for youth development
  • Organizing community service projects
  • Upholding the values of the Scout Oath and Law
  • Training young leaders for tomorrow
  • Engaging in disaster preparedness and response initiatives
